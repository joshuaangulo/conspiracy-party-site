The Premise

A Game of Belief

Equal parts dinner party and parlour mystery. Each guest arrives holding a single theory — assigned in advance — to defend before the assembled company.

You may believe it completely. You may find it perfectly preposterous. It makes no difference. Tonight there is but one question worth asking, and one mind clever enough to make us believe the answer.

The Rules of the Game

How the Evening Unfolds

I.

Your Theory Arrives Early

Your assignment is delivered well in advance. Study it. Live with it. Come prepared — this is no game for the unrehearsed.

II.

Any Medium You Can Master

Slides, film, song, props, exhibits of dubious provenance — present however you please. Craftsmanship will be noticed.

III.

Mind the Clock

Each presentation is permitted exactly ⟨ X ⟩ minutes. The clock is impartial, and unkind.

IV.

The Company Decides

When all have spoken, a vote is taken. The most convincing mind wins the prize. The rest receive our sincere admiration.

Come prepared. The most convincing case always carries the room.
